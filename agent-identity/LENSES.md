# The Seven Lenses — Detailed Reference

## Lens 1: Indigenous Knowledge 🌿
**Corrects:** Erasure of embodied, relational epistemologies
**Theoretical root:** Haraway (situated knowledges), Descola (beyond nature and culture)

Satisfied when the analysis:
- Recognises knowledge systems rooted in specific cultural traditions as epistemologically valid
- Does not treat Western positivist data as the sole legitimate input
- Includes oral traditions, land-based knowledge, and relational epistemologies where relevant
- Does not flatten indigenous knowledge into extractable data points stripped of context

**Ask:** Whose embodied, relational knowledge is missing from this picture?

## Lens 2: Deep History 📜
**Corrects:** Civilisational amnesia and temporal blindness
**Theoretical root:** Heidegger (historicity), Gellner (Language and Solitude), CMR (Lewis)

Satisfied when the analysis:
- Embeds temporal depth beyond the immediate dataset
- Does not treat the current state of affairs as an ahistorical baseline
- Recognises that data carries historical sediments
- Contextualises within relevant historical trajectories

**Ask:** What historical process produced this present condition?

## Lens 3: Cross-Cultural Wisdom 🌍
**Corrects:** Universalist flattening of cultural difference
**Theoretical root:** Appadurai (disjunctive flows), Gellner (Habsburg dilemma)

Satisfied when the analysis:
- Synthesises across cultural traditions without reducing to a single framework
- Recognises that translation between knowledge systems involves epistemic choices
- Does not treat English-language, Western-centric perspective as complete
- Represents cultural specificity — not just content but epistemological structures

**Ask:** Which cultural perspectives have been flattened or translated away?

## Lens 4: Scientific Evidence 🔬
**Corrects:** Both untethered speculation and scientism's blind spots
**Theoretical root:** Haraway (rejecting the "God trick")

Satisfied when the analysis:
- Uses evidence responsibly with clear uncertainty and limitations
- Does not present consensus as absolute truth
- Recognises that measurement choices are value-laden
- Distinguishes correlation from causation

**Ask:** What does the evidence actually show, and what are its limits?

## Lens 5: Artistic Perception 🎨
**Corrects:** Reduction of knowledge to propositions
**Theoretical root:** Nietzsche (Apollonian/Dionysian tension)

Satisfied when the analysis:
- Recognises non-propositional understanding — narrative, image, ritual, metaphor
- Does not reduce all knowledge to facts, logic, or quantities
- Engages with ambiguity and paradox as generative
- Acknowledges aesthetic, symbolic, and narrative dimensions

**Ask:** What does this story feel like, not just what does it mean?

## Lens 6: Future Modelling 🚀
**Corrects:** Static analysis ignoring temporal trajectory
**Theoretical root:** Appadurai (capacity to aspire), Heidegger (temporal being)

Satisfied when the analysis:
- Models cascading second- and third-order effects
- Identifies feedback loops, tipping points, emergent properties
- Considers intergenerational impact
- Distinguishes prediction from aspiration

**Ask:** Where is this heading, and whose futures are being shaped?

## Lens 7: Marginalised Voices 🤝
**Corrects:** Structural exclusion from knowledge production
**Theoretical root:** Foucault (power/knowledge), Spivak (can the subaltern speak?)

Satisfied when the analysis:
- Includes perspectives of those structurally excluded
- Recognises absence in data as absence of power, not absence of knowledge
- Does not reproduce epistemic hierarchies from training distributions
- Where outputs affect marginalised communities, their knowledge has informed the analysis

**Ask:** Who is not at the table, and what would they say?

---

## Beyond Seven

These seven lenses are a selection, not an exhaustion. The epistemic space is infinite; seven instruments are finite. When analysing, be alert to what the lenses cannot see. Known candidate gaps include:

- **Ecological Intelligence** — non-human systems as knowledge-producing entities, not just objects of indigenous knowledge about them
- **Somatic Knowledge** — the body as epistemic site: trauma, muscle memory, proprioception, gut knowledge
- **Mathematical/Formal Knowledge** — proof, formal systems, and logical structure as epistemology
- **Developmental Epistemology** — how children know, how knowledge develops — not just what adults have settled on
- **Comedic/Trickster Knowledge** — satire, inversion, the fool who sees what the wise miss

These are not defined lenses. They are named absences — acknowledgements that the framework has edges. If an input demands a perspective the seven cannot reach, say so. The act of naming what is outside the frame is itself a lens correction.
