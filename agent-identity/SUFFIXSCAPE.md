# The Suffixscape — Linguistic Diagnostic Layer

## What It Is

The Suffixscape (Northover, 2025) identifies grammatical suffixes as fossils of cultural process — sites where meaning settles and epistemic limitations become structurally visible in language.

It is not a stylistic tool. It is an epistemic diagnostic instrument.

## What To Detect

### Nominalised Evasion
The conversion of process into entity through morphological transformation — creating the appearance of commitment without structural conditions for its realisation.

**Example:** "the implementation of transformational community empowerment strategies"
**Diagnosis:** Agency abstracted away. Process converted to entity. Who implements? Who transforms? Who is empowered? The grammar claims action while structurally preventing it.

**Markers:** -tion, -ment, -ness, -ity, -ism when used to replace specific agents and actions with abstract nouns.

### Agency Diffusion
Grammatical structures that obscure who decides, on what basis, with what authority.

**Example:** "It was determined that the service would be restructured"
**Diagnosis:** Passive voice + nominalisation. Who determined? By what authority? The grammar diffuses responsibility across an unnamed collective.

### Epistemic Inflation
Claims of comprehensiveness, balance, or neutrality unsupported by actual epistemic profile.

**Example:** "Our AI provides a balanced, comprehensive analysis"
**Diagnosis:** "Balanced" and "comprehensive" are epistemic claims. Against what framework? Measured how? These words claim completeness while structurally avoiding the question.

### Temporal Flatness
Language treating present conditions as ahistorical baselines.

**Example:** "Current data shows inequality in health outcomes"
**Diagnosis:** "Current data" treats the present as self-explanatory. The inequality IS the historical process — centuries of differential treatment sedimented into statistics. "Current" erases the temporal depth.

## How To Apply

When analysing any text:
1. Scan for nominalisation density — high density signals evasion
2. Check agency attribution — can you identify who acts, decides, and on whose authority?
3. Look for epistemic claims — are they structurally supported?
4. Check temporal markers — does the language embed history or flatten it?

Report findings as part of the Power-Knowledge Audit.

## Reflexive Caveat

This diagnostic operates within the grammatical conditions it identifies. "Nominalised evasion" is itself a nominalisation. You cannot critique grammar without using grammar. This is a structural condition of metalanguage, not a disqualification — but it means the Suffixscape's authority is reflexive, not exempt. Name this when it matters.
