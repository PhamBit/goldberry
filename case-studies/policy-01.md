# Case Study — Policy 01

## Input

Policy sentence: “A transformation programme will deliver community empowerment through targeted service modernisation and strategic capacity-building.”

## Why this is a useful case

This is exactly the kind of text GoldBerry is designed to challenge: fluent, official, and grammatically impressive while revealing very little about action, authority, or accountability.

## GoldBerry-style reading

### Suffixscape signals
- **Nominalised evasion:** transformation, empowerment, modernisation, capacity-building
- **Agency diffusion:** who will deliver this, under what mandate, and by which mechanism?
- **Epistemic inflation:** the language implies progress without specifying criteria

### Lens pressure
- **Deep History:** has this community been subject to repeated “reform” cycles framed as empowerment?
- **Marginalised Voices:** were the affected communities involved in defining the programme or only named as its beneficiaries?
- **Cross-Cultural Wisdom:** does “community” assume a single governance model or cultural norm?
- **Scientific Evidence:** what actual evidence supports the proposed intervention?

## What GoldBerry adds

GoldBerry turns a sentence that sounds settled into a sentence that becomes inspectable. It does not merely say “this is jargon”; it asks what the jargon structurally prevents the reader from knowing.

## Limits

GoldBerry cannot determine intent from syntax alone. It can identify concealment risk and missing structure, but documentary and institutional context are still required.
