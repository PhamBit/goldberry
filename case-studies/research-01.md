# Case Study — Research 01

## Input

Abstract summary: A study reports improved learning outcomes from an AI tutoring system tested in two elite schools over six weeks. The abstract emphasises statistically significant gains and suggests broad promise for educational transformation.

## Baseline summary style

A conventional summary is likely to report:
- what the system did
- that outcomes improved
- that the findings are promising

## GoldBerry-style additions

A GoldBerry analysis would be expected to ask:
- Are two elite schools representative of broader educational settings?
- What labour, infrastructure, and cultural assumptions are built into the intervention?
- What forms of learning are counted, and which are excluded?
- How might this shape future educational inequality if adopted at scale?
- What student, teacher, or parent perspectives are not present in the abstract?

## What GoldBerry adds

- **Scientific Evidence:** significance is not the same as generalisability
- **Cross-Cultural Wisdom:** educational success is not culturally neutral
- **Future Modelling:** scaling effects may widen inequity rather than reduce it
- **Artistic Perception:** learning is not only measurable performance; it also involves meaning, confidence, and relational atmosphere

## Limits

GoldBerry does not invalidate positive findings. It broadens the frame around what the findings mean, for whom, and under what assumptions.
