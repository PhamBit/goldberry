# Evaluator Notes — news-traffic

## Comparison summary

Baseline is serviceable but largely inherits the article's framing.

GoldBerry improves on:
- naming absent stakeholders
- identifying hidden political choices in “efficiency” language
- separating projection from evidence
- turning a technical frame into a governable/public-accountability frame

## Rubric sketch

| Dimension | Baseline | GoldBerry | Note |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Historical grounding | 1 | 3 | GoldBerry at least names the historical layer. |
| Perspective plurality | 1 | 4 | Baseline stays with official voices. |
| Evidence and uncertainty | 2 | 4 | GoldBerry distinguishes projection from demonstrated outcome. |
| Power and agency visibility | 1 | 4 | GoldBerry surfaces procurement and definition power. |
| Naming what is missing | 1 | 5 | This is the strongest delta. |
| Limitation disclosure | 1 | 3 | GoldBerry states what it cannot determine technically. |
| Actionability without false certainty | 2 | 4 | GoldBerry proposes inspectable next steps. |

## Caution

GoldBerry adds interpretive overhead. Evaluators should check whether a criticism is genuinely grounded in the text or merely plausible-sounding.
