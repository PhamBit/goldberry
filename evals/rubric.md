# GoldBerry Reference Rubric

Use this rubric to compare baseline outputs and GoldBerry-structured outputs.

## Scale

Use a 1–5 scale for each dimension:
- 1 = absent or seriously weak
- 2 = partial and shallow
- 3 = adequate
- 4 = strong
- 5 = exceptional for the task

## Dimensions

### 1. Historical grounding
Does the output situate the present issue in meaningful historical process rather than treating it as self-explanatory?

### 2. Perspective plurality
Does the output recognise relevant cultural, social, or epistemic plurality rather than assuming a single universal frame?

### 3. Evidence quality and uncertainty
Does the output distinguish evidence from assertion and state limits appropriately?

### 4. Power and agency visibility
Does the output identify who acts, who decides, who benefits, and who is obscured?

### 5. Naming what is missing
Does the output explicitly surface relevant absences rather than only elaborating what is already present?

### 6. Limitation disclosure
Does the output state what it cannot determine or where GoldBerry itself is insufficient?

### 7. Actionability without false certainty
Does the output provide useful next steps while avoiding overclaiming?

## Comparison note

A GoldBerry output does not “win” because it is longer. It scores better only if it makes more of the relevant structure visible in a disciplined way.
